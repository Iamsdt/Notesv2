# **Company Leave, Work & Performance Policy**

## **1. Introduction & Scope**

This policy outlines the mandatory rules governing work hours, leave management, performance expectations, and the structure for rewards and penalties.  
Adherence to this policy is a condition of employment. Non-compliance will result in disciplinary action.

---

## **2. Working Hours**

- **Standard Hours:** Official working hours are **10:00 AM to 7:00 PM** (8-hour workday).
- **Working Schedule:**
    - **Monday – Friday:** Standard working days.
    - **Saturday (First Half):** Mandatory working session dedicated to clearing pending sprint tasks and resolving sprint-related bugs.
    - **Saturday (Second Half):** Optional. Employees may use this time for self-selected projects or professional development activities.

---

## **3. Leave Management Policy**

### **3.1. Annual Paid Leave**

- Employees are entitled to **12 days** of paid annual leave per year (subject to future adjustment).
- Leave entitlement is calculated on a **pro-rata basis** for new joiners.
### **3.2. Unused Leave (Carry Forward)**
- A maximum of **5 unused leave days** can be carried forward to the next calendar year.
- Any unused leave balance exceeding 5 days will be forfeited without compensation.

### **3.3. Leave Options**
- Employees may opt for **first-half or second-half leave** on statutory days, subject to managerial approval.

### **3.4. Public Holidays**
- Employees are entitled to **7 floating public holidays** per year. These must be selected from the company’s approved list and approved in advance by management.

---

## **4. Leave Approval Process**

- **Advance Notice:** All leave requests must be planned and approved in advance by your designated manager.
- **Exceptions:** Only verified, documented emergencies are exempt. Falsifying an emergency will result in severe disciplinary action.
- **Right to Refuse:** The company reserves the right to deny leave based on business needs, project deadlines, or policy violations.

---
## **5. Employee Conduct During Leave**
- **Mandatory Availability:** Employees on leave must remain available by phone for urgent matters.
- Failure to respond to critical communications during leave will be treated as a serious breach of duty.

---
## **6. Customer Priority Response Team (CPRT)**
- **Absolute Availability:** CPRT members must be available **24/7/365**. This is a non-negotiable condition for being part of the team.
- **Team Rotation & Structure:**
    - The CPRT operates on a rotating basis (weekly or monthly) as determined by management.
    - **Main Point of Contact (2):** At least one Frontend specialist and one Backend specialist.
    - **Second Point of Contact (2):** At least one Frontend specialist and one Backend specialist for escalation.
- **Accountability:** Failure to respond to an incident while on duty will result in immediate penalties and a formal performance review.

---
## **7. Compensation for Extra Duty & Night-Time Incentives**
- **Official Overtime:** Assigned night work and work on holidays will be compensated according to the company’s separate rewards structure.
- **Night-Time Development:** Employees investing time in learning new technologies or working on personal/professional development projects during night hours may receive recognition or rewards, subject to management verification.

---
## **8. Performance, Evaluation, Rewards & Penalties**
### **8.1. Core Principle of Evaluation**
Performance is the key metric for all decisions related to rewards, penalties, WFH privileges, and career progression.  
It is measured based on the **successful and timely completion of sprint tasks**.

---

### **8.2. Rewards**
All rewards are based on **measurable high performance**.
- **Work From Home (WFH):**    
    - WFH is a **privilege**, not a right.
    - Granted for sustained, exceptional performance and requires explicit management approval.
    - Can be revoked at any time based on performance or business needs.
- **Cash Rewards:**
    - Awarded for resolving **Customer P1 bugs** within defined SLAs.
- **Sprint & Performance Rewards:**
    - **Sprint Hero:**
        - Awarded **weekly** to the **top 3 performers** in the sprint.
        - Each winner will receive  Work From Home (WFH)** as a reward.
    - **Spotlight Star:**
        - Awarded **monthly** to the **most consistent high performer**.
        - Includes gifts, vouchers, or other incentives as determined by management.
    - **Innovation Award:**
        - Recognizes employees who dedicate time to innovation — e.g., building new libraries, creating tools, or adding new features/projects.
        - **Two employees** will be selected **monthly** for **gift vouchers or equivalent recognition**.

---

### **8.3. Penalties & Performance Management**

- **P1 Bug Penalty:** Failure to resolve assigned Customer P1 bugs within the SLA will result in financial or disciplinary penalties.
- **Lack of Collaboration:** Obstructing team progress or refusing to collaborate effectively will be treated as a violation.
- **Poor Communication:** Unclear, delayed, or unprofessional communication with team members or management will be penalized.
- **Casual or Negligent Behavior:** Lack of seriousness or commitment toward assigned tasks will not be tolerated.

- **Underperformance (Below 100%):**
    - Employees completing less than 100% of assigned sprint tasks must attend a **mandatory performance review call** with management.
- **Consistent Underperformance:**
    - Considered a serious red flag indicating inability to meet job standards.
    - Will trigger **formal disciplinary actions**, which may include termination.
