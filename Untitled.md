## **Executive Summary**

  

This Constituent Off-boarding and Role Change Policy establishes Hire10x's comprehensive framework for managing employment terminations and role transitions to protect organizational assets and maintain security posture. The policy defines mandatory procedures for revoking access rights, recovering company assets, and ensuring smooth knowledge transfer during employee departures and role changes. All terminations and role changes are treated as time-sensitive security events requiring immediate action to prevent unauthorized access and protect customer data and intellectual property.

## **1. Introduction and Purpose**

  

### **1.1 Principle**

  

Effective constituent off-boarding and role change management are critical components of Hire10x's security and operational excellence programs. This policy ensures systematic processes for managing employment transitions while protecting organizational assets, maintaining security posture, and supporting business continuity.

  

### **1.2 Purpose**

  

This Constituent Off-boarding and Role Change Policy aims to:

  

- Establish systematic procedures for employment terminations and role transitions

- Ensure timely revocation of access rights and system privileges

- Protect organizational assets and intellectual property during transitions

- Maintain comprehensive audit trails and documentation

- Support smooth knowledge transfer and business continuity

- Minimize security risks and unauthorized access exposure

- Ensure regulatory compliance and legal requirement adherence

- Provide consistent and professional departure experiences

  

### **1.3 Policy Objectives**

  

- Achieve 100% completion of off-boarding procedures within established timelines

- Maintain zero unauthorized access incidents from departed constituents

- Ensure complete asset recovery and inventory accuracy

- Provide comprehensive audit trails for all termination and role change activities

- Support positive departure experiences and alumni relationship maintenance

- Minimize business disruption and operational impact during transitions

- Continuous improvement of off-boarding and role change processes

## **3. Definitions and Glossary**

  

**Off-boarding:** The process of managing an employee's departure from the organization, including access revocation, asset recovery, and knowledge transfer.

  

**Role Change:** A modification in an employee's job responsibilities, department, or access requirements that necessitates permission review and adjustment.

  

**Access Revocation:** The immediate removal of system access, credentials, and privileges upon termination or role change.

  

**Asset Recovery:** The process of collecting and inventorying all company-owned equipment, materials, and intellectual property from departing employees.

  

**Knowledge Transfer:** The systematic documentation and handover of job responsibilities, processes, and institutional knowledge to ensure business continuity.

  

**Privilege Creep:** The gradual accumulation of access rights and permissions beyond what is necessary for current job responsibilities.

  

**Principle of Least Privilege:** A security concept requiring that individuals be granted only the minimum access necessary to perform their job functions.

  

**De-provisioning:** The technical process of removing user accounts, access rights, and system privileges across all organizational systems.

  

**Exit Interview:** A formal meeting conducted with departing employees to gather feedback and ensure completion of departure procedures.

  

**Separation Agreement:** A legal document outlining the terms and conditions of an employee's departure, including confidentiality and non-compete obligations.

  

**Alumni Network:** Former employees who maintain positive relationships with the organization and may provide referrals or business opportunities.

  

**Notice Period:** The advance notification time provided before an employee's departure, allowing for transition planning and preparation.

  

**Garden Leave:** A period where an employee remains on payroll but is restricted from working or accessing company systems before their departure.

## **5. Off-boarding Process Framework**

  

### **5.1 Process Initiation and Notification**

  

**Notification Requirements:**

- Immediate notification to HR and IT teams upon departure decision

- Security team notification for time-sensitive access revocation

- Manager notification for knowledge transfer and team coordination

- Executive notification for senior-level departures

- Legal team notification for potential risk or compliance issues

  

**Timeline Standards:**

- Voluntary departures: Minimum 2 weeks advance notice

- Involuntary terminations: Immediate process initiation

- Role changes: 1 week advance notice for access planning

- Emergency terminations: Immediate response within 2 hours

- Executive departures: 30-day transition planning period

  

### **5.2 Risk Assessment and Classification**

  

**Risk Categories:**

- **High Risk:** Senior executives, security personnel, developers with sensitive access

- **Medium Risk:** Managers, customer-facing roles, financial personnel

- **Low Risk:** Administrative staff, junior employees, limited system access

- **Critical Risk:** Emergency terminations, security violations, competitive departures

  

**Assessment Factors:**

- Level of system access and privileges

- Sensitivity of information accessed

- Customer relationship and business impact

- Reason for departure and circumstances

- Competitive risk and intellectual property exposure

  

### **5.3 Process Coordination and Management**

  

**Cross-Functional Team:**

- HR business partner as process coordinator

- IT administrator for technical de-provisioning

- Security officer for risk assessment and monitoring

- Direct manager for knowledge transfer and team coordination

- Legal counsel for compliance and documentation

  

**Communication Protocols:**

- Secure communication channels for sensitive information

- Regular status updates and milestone reporting

- Escalation procedures for issues and delays

- Stakeholder notification and coordination

- Documentation and audit trail maintenance

## **6. Immediate Access Revocation Procedures**

  

### **6.1 Access Inventory and Assessment**

  

**System Access Review:**

- Enterprise applications and software platforms

- Cloud services and infrastructure access (AWS, GCP, Azure)

- Communication systems (email, messaging, video conferencing)

- Development tools and code repositories

- Customer systems and third-party applications

  

**Privilege Assessment:**

- Administrative and elevated privileges

- Shared accounts and service credentials

- API keys and authentication tokens

- VPN and remote access capabilities

- Physical access badges and keycards

  

### **6.2 De-provisioning Execution**

  

**Immediate Actions (Within 2 Hours):**

1. Disable primary user accounts and authentication

2. Revoke VPN and remote access capabilities

3. Suspend email and communication system access

4. Disable cloud service console and API access

5. Deactivate physical access badges and keycards

  

**Standard Timeline (Within 24 Hours):**

1. Complete application and system access review

2. Remove from distribution lists and shared resources

3. Transfer ownership of documents and files

4. Update emergency contact and delegation assignments

5. Coordinate shared credential rotation and updates

  

### **6.3 Verification and Validation**

  

**Access Verification:**

- System-by-system access confirmation

- Failed login attempt monitoring and analysis

- Shared credential identification and rotation

- Physical access testing and validation

- Third-party system notification and coordination

  

**Documentation Requirements:**

- Complete access revocation checklist

- Timestamp documentation for all actions

- Exception identification and resolution tracking

- Manager and HR confirmation and sign-off

- Security team validation and approval
